Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 10, 2026

Overview

NoLag is designed with privacy as a core principle. The app runs entirely on your Mac, and your data stays on your Mac. We do not collect, transmit, or store any personal information on external servers.

Data Collection

NoLag does not collect any personal data. Specifically:

  • No account creation or sign-in is required
  • No analytics or tracking tools are used
  • No personal information is transmitted over the internet
  • No cookies are used
  • No advertising SDKs are included

Network Connections

NoLag measures network latency by establishing brief TCP connections to hosts you configure (e.g. 8.8.8.8). These connections are used solely to measure round-trip time and are closed immediately after each measurement. No data is sent or received through these connections beyond the TCP handshake.

Host Configuration

The list of hosts you configure is stored locally on your Mac using standard macOS preferences. This data is never transmitted to any server.

Data Storage & Deletion

All data — ping history, host configuration, and preferences — is stored locally on your Mac. Ping history is kept in memory only and is not persisted to disk. Uninstalling NoLag removes all app data.

Third-Party Services

NoLag does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or cloud storage services.

Children's Privacy

NoLag does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13. Since no data is collected at all, NoLag is compliant with COPPA and similar regulations.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever update this privacy policy, changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. Since NoLag collects no data, we don't anticipate significant changes.

Contact
